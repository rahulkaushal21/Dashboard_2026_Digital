5. CROSS-PERSPECTIVE WON/VALUE CHECK (per-deal, conservative). For each open opp, check

5b. WON-LAG — when the email says confirmed but the sheet hasn't caught up.
   Status/Won for sheet deals is driven by the Quotes tab, so a client who approves by

   until someone edits the sheet. Do NOT force the sheet row to Won. Instead, for any

   rfq_status='Approved — verbal go-ahead' (or 'Won — email-confirmed') and, for
   email-origin deals, win_probability>=90. The dashboard then auto-raises a
   "⚠ REVIEW URGENT — client confirmed in email but still Open" flag on that deal.
   List every deal you flagged this way, with the quote as evidence.

   Review flags are auto-computed on the Opportunities page (in getOpportunities, for
   still-open, not-Lost deals) — you don't write them, your writes drive them:
   - ⚠ REVIEW URGENT (won-lag) — email-origin deal marked approved / >=90% (step 5b).
   - Business Type mismatch — booked/existing client tagged pure "New" (col P).
   - ⚠ Stale — no dated movement in >21 days. Enriching in step 4 clears this.

      clean company (now from the FULL subject when there's no delimiter) and gist. Never
      leave company_name blank.
   e. SOURCE_DATE HONESTY. Only bump source_date to a newer date when there is a GENUINE
      recent client message in that thread. Never batch-bump. If a deal's newest real
      activity is months old, leave the old date so it reads as stale (a bumped date makes a
      dead deal masquerade as active and breaks the date filter). To repair: reset
      source_date = first_date for any stale deal whose source_date was pushed >30 days past
      first_date with no real recent thread.
   f. STALE REVIEW. List open deals with no real movement in >90 days; confirm still-live or
      mark Lost. Don't let 2025 leads sit Open forever inflating the pipeline.
